# The Length

It's ~34 km, not 43. This is now ironclad — four independent things agree:
  - A firsthand 6,364-point GPS recording = 34.31 km (a recording that dense cannot be "missing switchbacks").       
  - The Ministry of Culture's OSM track = 36 km.
  - Your camp elevations match topo at the GPS camp coordinates within 10–30 m.
  - Your "50% at the pass" note = km 16.4 of 34 km = 48%.

The famous "43 km" is just the romanticized figure (sources range 39–52 km with no consensus; operator per-day     
numbers sum to 52). Day-by-day from the GPS camps: D1 11 km → D2 7.7 km (over the pass) → D3 10.6 km → D4 4.7 km.  
I'll anchor the page to the accurate ~34 km and note "often cited as 43 km" so it still matches searches.
